Emerging Applications of Artificial Intelligence in 2019 (Ballroom C)

Agenda:

Details about speaker and presentation.

Seminar Speaker: Yi-Min Wang, Ph.D.
CVP, CTO of Microsoft AI

Yi-Min leads the R&D efforts in building AI-powered solutions that aim at transforming enterprise business processes. In 2017, his org incubated Customer Care Intelligence Virtual Agent and Sales Intelligence Predictive Lead Scoring, both of which have since become Dynamics 365 products.
As Managing Director of Microsoft Research Technologies (MSR-T), his team shipped the Skype Translator to help break human language barriers and the Word Flow keyboard technology that broke two Guinness World Records. He led a Cybersecurity research team to develop anti-malware technologies to protect Windows customers. He invented the Strider HoneyMonkey - the first automated system to patrol the Web and hunt for malicious websites that exploit zero-day vulnerabilities. The HoneyMonkey technique has become a de facto standard for both the security industry and the search engine industry.
Received PhD in Computer Engineering from UIUC and is IEEE Fellow

Presentation Topic: AI in Natural Language Processing and Computer Vision
Abstract: Deep Learning has revolutionized Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Computer Vision (CV) in the past few years. In this talk, I will show examples of how these technologies have been applied to real-world business process transformation scenarios. I will also describe a few emerging applications in NLP and in the intersection of NLP and CV.

Seminar Speaker: Yinhai Wang, Ph.D., P.E.
Director and Professor, University of Washington


Professor in transportation engineering at both Civil and Environmental Engineering and Electrical and Computer Engineering Departments of the University of Washington
Director of Pacific Northwest Transportation Consortium, USDOT University Transportation Center for Federal Region 10 (Alaska, Idaho, Oregon, and Washington)
President of Transportation and Development Institute at American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE)
Winner of the ASCE transportation engineering best paper award, Chinese Overseas Transportation Association's outstanding leadership award, and Institute of Transportation Engineers' Innovation in Education Award


Presentation Topic: AI for Smarter Transportation and Infrastructure Systems
Abstract: Transportation has been evolving quickly due to the emerging technologies, such as IoT, Connected and Autonomous Vehicles, and new business models, such as ride-hailing services and bike-sharing programs. These technologies and business models will disrupt transportation industry, from car ownership to the ways of travel and transportation infrastructure operations. AI plays a critical role in processing the tremendous amount of data harvested from traffic detectors, IoT network deployments, and wide use of navigation systems. Speaker will share his vision and research on AI applications for smarter transportation and infrastructure systems.


Seminar Speaker: Jennifer Hadlock, MD
Assistant Professor at the Institute of System Biology
Jennifer Hadlock, MD is an Assistant Professor at the Institute of System Biology. Her lab investigates the integration of clinical information into systems biology at scale, including machine learning across millions of electronic health records. Previously, she worked at Microsoft on technologies for natural language processing and digital imaging, which have been used by hundreds of millions of people around the world. Jennifer has a BS in Mathematics and Computer Science, and earned her MD at the University of Washington School of Medicine.
Presentation Topic: Machine learning with electronics health records
Abstract: A vast volume of clinical data is generated from many sources, including research studies and electronic health records (EHRs). At the same time, rapid advances in cloud computing and machine learning are providing powerful new methods for data analysis. This presentation illustrates a few of the emerging opportunities, challenges and potential pitfalls that arise when analyzing clinical data at scale. Examples will include new approaches toward advancing sepsis care, and a study on adolescent cognitive brain development, which integrates genetic data, MRI imaging and patient-reported experiences.


Seminar Speaker: Shankar Vaidyanathan
CEO and Founder of Noonum.ai

Shankar is the Founder and CEO of Noonum, a Seattle-based fintech startup.
He has more than 20 years of experience at Microsoft as an Engineering Leader in the Cloud, Data, and Machine Learning space, driving the success of Internet of Things, Bing Advertising, and Visual Studio.
He holds more than a dozen US and International patents and a few more under process.
He has a graduate degree in Computer Science with a thesis on model-based machine learning


Presentation Topic: AI in Finance
Abstract: Noonum, a local fintech startup, solves information overload by summarizing and contextualizing unstructured data, powered by the connected intelligence of a continually evolving semantic knowledge graph generated using Artificial Intelligence and Natural Language Processing. This talk covers some of the challenges in machine's understanding of the English language, followed by a powerful demo of the solution in action.


Seminar Speaker: T. Augustine Lo, Esq.
Associate, DORSEY & WHITNEY LLP

Attorney at Dorsey & Whitney LLP on U.S. sanctions and export controls, customs matters, and commercial litigation
Previously worked on customs enforcement matters for the Office of Chief Counsel of U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP), a federal agency in the U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS)
Harvard (BA), Cambridge (M. Phil), and NYU (J.D.)


Presentation Topic: US-China Disputes and Effect on Cross-Border Cooperation
Abstract: The U.S. has long complained to China's leaders about China's policies on intellectual property protection, foreign trade, government subsidies for certain industries, cybersecurity, and the growing power of the People's Liberation Army (PLA) as it continues to modernize and expand. The world's two largest economies are now locked in a growing trade war of tariffs, counter-tariffs and other retaliatory measures that have damaged both importers and exporters. Since taking office, the Trump Administration has chosen to escalate the confrontation of China in many ways that are disrupting trade and investment, research and development, joint ventures, global supply chains, higher education, and immigration, among other areas. One of the most visible clashes centers around the Administration's actions against Huawei Technologies, China's most prominent technology company and the world's largest telecommunications equipment producer. Augustine will discuss these developments with a focus on the potential impacts for Chinese American engineers and their families. As an attorney in Dorsey's national security practice group, Augustine has "hands-on" experience with many of these issues, as well as prior government enforcement experience as an attorney for U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP), an agency of the U.S. Department of Homeland Security.

UW Botanic Gardens Tram Tour

Saturday, September 14, 2019

Experience the beauty of over 200 acres on a guided open-air tram tour through the UW Botanic Gardens Arboretum - Seattle's outdoor living museum. This one-hour narrated ride winds you through the 80-year-old Arboretum's woodlands and wetlands: -Learn about plants from nearly 100 countries!-Discover the Arboretum's connection with horses.-Sample a 16th century beverage made from the root of a woody plant. FAQ What about inclement weather? Tours will run rain or shine. We ask that you check the weather forecast and dress accordingly if inclement weather is predicted. Will locals enjoy the tour as well, or is it mostly for tourists? Absolutely! While you may be familiar with the Arboretum you will also have the opportunity to discover new things about Seattle! Additionally, this tour is an excellent way to entertain out-of-town guests. Are tickets refundable? All ticket sales are final. If there are extenuating circumstances please contact tramtour@uw.edu, but please note this is answered Monday through Friday between 9am and 5pm. Is the tour suitable for children? Including children is up to the discretion of the adults accompanying the children. All attendees pay the general ticket price regardless of age; however, babe in arms is free. Is the tour wheelchair and stroller accessible? Unfortunately, no. The tram does not have room for a wheelchair or stroller. If you are able to leave them in the Visitors Center and are able to sit on the Tram then yes, you can tour! Where does the Tour depart exactly? Washington Park Arboretum, 2300 Arboretum Drive East, Seattle, WA 98122. Check in at the information desk located in the Graham Visitors Center anytime after 10AM. Tours will begin boarding about 5 minutes before departure. Is there public parking available nearby? There are multiple parking lots for free near the Visitors Center. On a busy day these can fill early! We also recommend taking public transportation and/or using resources such as Uber, Lyft or bike share programs. We are about a 20 minute walk for the UW Light Rail Station. Specific parking and public transportation directions will be included in the meeting location details after purchasing tickets. How many people will be on the tour? Each tour is capped at 10 people per tour guide. Do you offer private tours? Yes! Private Tours (both walking and tram tours) are available year round. To schedule a private tour, please email tramtour@uw.edu DISCLAIMER: The UW Botanic Gardens Arboretum Tram Tour (the Tour) takes place in a 13-passenger tram that has lap belts but no side walls. Tours can travel up to 10 mph and make frequent stops as well as traveling over speed bumps and taking sharp turns.
